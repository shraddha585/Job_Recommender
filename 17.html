What is Manager?

Managers play an important role in the overall success of a company. They are responsible for leading a team of employees to meet goals and achieve performance metrics

Types of Manager

1. Top managers

Top managers are usually involved with their company's overall strategy. They may oversee many departments. They are also responsible for making sure that different tasks and projects align with the overarching mission of their company.

2. General managers

General managers are responsible for a variety of management tasks, including overseeing product production. They typically set goals for their employees and create product plans. Usually, a general manager's overall goal is to increase their company's revenue.

3. Line managers

Line managers are responsible for achieving specific outputs. Typically, their responsibilities relate to their company's products or services. Line managers often communicate with a company's upper management and report results to them.

4. Team managers

Team managers, which may be referred to as supervisors, are responsible for overseeing specific groups or functions of an organisation. They may be tasked with supervising projects or keeping teams on track. Like line managers, team managers also report results to a company's upper management.

How to become a manager

Students right after class 10 can choose Commerce (with or without maths) to start preparing for undergraduate courses that will help them to become an HR Manager in India. The following section discusses the step-by-step process to become an HR Manager after class 12th.

• Graduation: After completion of class 10+2, one will have to clear the entrance examination and complete a 3 years undergraduate course. The graduation course is for 3 years in India and then you will have to do an internship.

• Post Graduation: After completion of UG Degree, the Candidate can do an internship for a year and then can pursue further education. Having a PG course will help the students to master the skills required to become successful HR Managers.

Diploma in Human Resource Management

A Diploma in Human Resource Management is a certificate-level course in the domain of human resource management. It is a 1-year course that studies managerial skills in relation to the effective management of humans as a resource.

Admission Process

• Diploma in human resource management course admissions are mostly based on a merit-based criterion.

• Students who must have passed 10+2 or equivalent examinations with a minimum of 50% can apply in various institutes and send applications to colleges/institutes.

BBA Human Resource Management

BBA Human Resource Management is a 3 year UG course that equips students with the knowledge of principles to handle the human resources of a company or any institution.

Admission Process

• Top colleges for the BBA human resource management course issue application form and selection is purely based on merit.

• Shortlisting and preparation of merit list are done on a merit basis, after that candidate is called for a personal interview round.

• For some of the top universities like Delhi University, the cut-off can be as high as up to 99% for admission to top colleges.

• Some colleges also conduct entrance examinations to give admissions to the BBA course.

MBA Human Resource

MBA in Human Resource Management is a 2-year PG course focusing on recruitment, management, and providing direction and guidance in organisations. This course offers training and knowledge in managing the workforce of an organisation.

PhD Human Resource Management

PhD in Human Resource Management could be a 3-5 year student program within the field of human resource management. The course includes various aspects such as organizational skills, training and developmental skills, decision-making skills and communication skills.

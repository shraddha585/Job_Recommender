What is an Architect

An architect is responsible for the design, planning, and supervision of the construction of buildings and other physical structures. They work closely with clients to understand their needs and objectives and create designs that meet their requirements while also adhering to building codes and regulations.

Types of Architects:

1. Residential Architect: A residential architect specializes in the design and planning of homes and residential buildings. They work closely with clients to understand their needs and preferences, and to create a design that meets their functional, aesthetic, and budgetary requirements. They may also provide guidance on energy-efficient building practices, as well as compliance with local building codes and regulations.

2. Commercial Architect: A commercial architect specializes in the design and planning of commercial buildings, such as office buildings, retail spaces, and hotels. They may also provide guidance on energy-efficient building practices, as well as compliance with local building codes and regulations.

3. Industrial Architect: An industrial architect specializes in the design of buildings and structures used in industrial and manufacturing processes. They create efficient and functional spaces that are safe for workers and that optimize production.

4. Interior Architect: An interior architect specializes in the design and planning of interior spaces, such as homes, offices, and retail spaces. They work closely with clients to create a design that meets their requirements while adhering to building codes and regulations.

5. Sustainable Architect: A sustainable architect designs buildings with a focus on minimizing their environmental impact. They consider factors such as energy efficiency, use of renewable materials, and waste reduction in their designs.

6. Restoration Architect: A restoration architect specializes in the preservation and restoration of historic buildings, monuments, and other cultural heritage sites. Restoration architects often work closely with historians, archaeologists, and other experts to ensure that the restoration process is respectful of the building's history and cultural significance.

7. Architectural Designer: An architectural designer specializes in the creative and technical aspects of architectural design. Architectural designers work closely with architects and clients to develop designs for various structures, including buildings, residences, and public spaces.

8. Landscape Architect: Landscape architects specialize in the design and planning of outdoor spaces, such as parks, gardens, and campuses. They may also provide guidance on sustainable and eco-friendly landscaping practices, such as the use of native plants and efficient irrigation systems.

9. Urban Planner: Urban planners work to create and implement plans for the development of cities, neighborhoods, and urban spaces, considering aspects such as infrastructure, transportation, and community development.

10. Naval Architect: Naval architects specialize in the design, construction, and repair of ships, boats, and other marine vessels. They use their expertise in engineering, physics, and materials science to create safe, efficient, and seaworthy vessels. Their work can range from designing small pleasure boats to large commercial and military vessels.

How to become an Architect?

Undergraduate Preparations: Some of the crucial steps that must be followed in order to be an Architect are as follows:

• Candidates need to clear the entrance exam in order to get admission in the undergraduate program such as JEE Mains, JEE Advanced, NATA, AAT etc are some of the top entrance exams.

• Candidates can pursue undergraduate courses in Architecture such as B.Arch, Bachelor of Architecture in Landscape Architecture, Bachelor of Planning, Bachelor of Engineering in Construction Technology and many more.

• Candidates should focus on designing models of the building, formulating construction blueprints and other physical structures of land and building.

• To pursue a higher degree, candidates must score a minimum aggregate of 60% or above in undergraduation.

• Candidates must go through the syllabus briefly in order to prepare for the entrance exam for post graduation

Post-Graduation Preparation: The key steps to be followed to become a after completing Undergraduate courses are,

• For getting into a post graduate program, candidates need to appear for the entrance exam such as CEEED, GATE etc.

• Candidates need to choose specialization in any area depending on their area of interest or knowledge and strength.

• In this course, on-field experience plays a very important role. So it’s important for the candidates to attend workshops and pursue internships to learn about the actual job role.

• Post completion of the course, candidates while working can also pursue higher degrees such as PhD to get a doctorate degree which would be very beneficial in landing a high scoring job.

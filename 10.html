FASHION DESIGNER


WHAT IS FASHION DESIGNER?

Fashion design is the art of applying design, aesthetics, clothing construction and natural beauty to clothing and its accessories. It is influenced by culture and different trends, and has varied over time and place. "A fashion designer creates clothing, including dresses, suits, pants, and skirts, and accessories like shoes and handbags, for consumers. He or she can specialize in clothing, accessory, or jewelry design, or may work in more than one of these areas."
Professional fashion designers create clothes, accessories and shoes. Depending on your position within an organisation, you may be expected to develop preliminary designs, produce final products, or handle any intermediary steps between the conception and production of designs.

Types of Fashion Designer

1. Apparel

Apparel designers focus on creating various clothing items for customers. Since there are many types of clothing pieces to create, apparel designers typically pursue specialties within this field. They often create different styles for consumers all over the world, from celebrity dresses to workers' uniforms.

2. Haute couture

These designers typically work for more high-profile and wealthier consumers. They often create customized and one-of-a-kind apparel according to each individual customers' needs and preferences. Haute couture designers usually have a smaller clientele, as they regularly work with the same customers to take their measurements and design clothing pieces for upcoming events according to the clients' specific style and preferences.

3. Ready-to-wear

Also known as pret-a-porter designers, these professionals create apparel for groups of people. These designers create outfits with the correct material, presentation and cut customers request and manufacture their clothes in smaller and more segmented groups. This ensures that the apparel fits the specifications of each customer without being considered a custom-made design.

4. Mass market

These designers create apparel items for a general and broad audience. Each item a mass-market designer creates undergoes a mass-production process and reaches the open market for sale to any customers interested in purchasing them. These clothes rarely have any limited editions or custom fitting options available to customers, as anyone willing to purchase the products can buy and wear them.

5. Footwear

Footwear designers create new shoes, like sneakers, flats or high heels. Many professionals who pursue this field can specialize in certain areas, like athletic or formal footwear. Employees who work in this field typically follow apparel trends to ensure they match well with the shoe designs they create. They may collaborate closely with apparel designers to build complementary designs. Some footwear designers may create custom designs for individual consumers, especially sneaker designers.

6. Accessory

These designers create jewelry, like bracelets, earrings and necklaces or accessory items like scarves, hats, handbags and glasses. Accessory designers are typically responsible for creating captivating and fashionable designs for items that aren't considered apparel or clothing. They typically visit design conventions or other related industry events to meet with manufacturers to purchase individual materials and pieces to build whole accessory designs.

7. Sportwear

Designers within this category typically make fashion pieces for athletic or daily wear. The fabrics they use often contain more comfortable and durable materials that can stretch easily and wick away any moisture or sweat when customers exercise. Both apparel and shoes designers often work within this category of designers. These designers need advanced knowledge of special finishes and performance textiles to create quality, long-lasting and durable designs.

8. Evening wear

Designers for this type of fashion typically focus on more luxurious styles, fabrics and materials. Customers typically wear these designs to elegant events. Because of this, the fabric used to make these pieces is often more expensive than most, so the revenue gained from product sales can be high. Common types of apparel these designers make include evening gowns, suits, tuxedos and wedding dresses.

9. Childrenswear

Childrenswear designers create shoes and apparel for kids of all ages, from infants to teenagers. They usually work with fewer fabrics and materials, since children-sized clothes are often smaller than other clothing items. Most of these items' designs also come with lists of materials, fabrics or other design choices designers can't use. For instance, designers shouldn't include detachable buttons on infants' or toddlers' clothes, as they could be choking hazards.

10. Limited edition

Limited edition designers typically create pieces for customers who prefer more exclusive apparel, shoes or accessories at lower prices than haute couture items. There are many popular brands with mass-produced items that may offer more exclusive product lines or collections with additional customization options for slightly higher prices than the rest of their mass-produced attire.

11. Classic wear

Those who design classic wear pieces usually create timeless designs that rarely go out of style. These designers create most of these items using high-quality fabrics or materials that are incredibly durable and long-lasting. Customers can also usually wear these pieces all year round, rather than only wearing them during certain fashion seasons.

12. Eco-fashion

These designers create apparel that's stylish, trendy and affordable all at once. Many of these pieces are also good for the environment, as most of the items or fabrics come from sourced materials. Designers usually make these items for customers who want to wear socially conscious and environmentally sound attire at a reasonable and affordable cost.

HOW TO BECOME FASHION DESIGNER

1. Complete your bachelor's degree

One of the basic requirements for a fashion designer is a bachelor's degree in fashion design, fashion merchandising or visual arts. After completing your 10+2, you require to prepare for entrance examinations for fashion designing colleges

2. Complete your master's degree

After completing your bachelor's degree, you can continue your education with a postgraduate degree, or you can gain some work experience before pursuing a master's degree. Some popular fashion designer courses at the postgraduate level include Master of Design (MDes), Master of Fashion Technology (MFTech) and Master of Fashion Management (MFM).

3. Create a portfolio of your designs

If you want to become a fashion designer, you are required to start developing a digital portfolio of your designs as early as possible. All of your sketches, collections and prototypes, including those you have developed as a part of your coursework, requires to be in your portfolio. Your digital portfolio demonstrates your skills, style and future potential to employers.

4. Create a fashion designer resume

After graduating from school, you require to gain hands-on experience in the fashion industry. You are required to bolster your portfolio with a strong fashion designer resume that sets you apart from other applicants. Your resume is required to include your educational background, professional experience , skills and links to your portfolio.

5. Gain hands-on experience in the fashion industry

Due to the competitiveness of the fashion industry, you are required to start your career with strategic internships. You can look for internship opportunities with established organisations or assist experienced fashion designers. This helps you gain practical job skills

, understand the industry as an insider and build professional networks.

Famous fashion designers who hire fashion design graduates include Rohit Bal, Manish Malhotra, Varun Behl, Sabyasachi Mukherjee and Raghuvendra Rathore.

6. Build a professional network

Networking is a crucial step in becoming a professional fashion designer. You require to be on the lookout for avenues to network with other fashion design aspirants, colleagues and established professional fashion designers. As such, you are required to attend all fashion shows, enter amateur contests, join professional groups and foster positive relationships with your colleagues and employers.

7. Stay abreast of fashion trends

Fashion trends change from season to season. As such, you require to stay abreast of recent trends and keep an eye out for upcoming trends. You can sharpen your instincts by attending fashion shows and reading fashion journals and magazines. When an opportunity presents itself, you are required to seize it in whatever capacity possible at the time.

• Bachelor of Design (BDes): the course covers fashion communication, product design, fashion design, textile design and industrial design.

• Bachelor of Fashion Technology (BFTech): the course covers the technical aspects of fashion design and the apparel manufacturing industry.

• Bachelor of Science in Fashion Design (BSc Fashion Design): the course covers specialised fields of garment design, interior design, leather design, footwear design and jewellery and accessory design.

• Bachelor of Science in Fashion Merchandising (BSc Fashion Merchandising): the course covers the marketing and merchandising aspects of the fashion industry.

• Master of Fashion Technology (MFTech): it is an advanced two-year post-graduate course that covers all the technical aspects of designing, with specialisations in garment designing and accessory designing.

• Master of Design (MDes): it is an advanced post-graduate degree that focuses on design elements and principles in various industries, including fashion design.
